From The Founder

It’s time we had a platform to advance kidney health

I started Signals because kidney disease is both deeply personal and widely overlooked. As a Navy veteran and VA patient-turned-entrepreneur, I’ve seen the toll it takes on patients, families, and healthcare systems—and how often these challenges remain invisible outside the kidney community. I wanted to build something that could change that.

Before Signals, I spent nearly a decade leading IKONA, a company I co-founded to reimagine patient education through virtual reality. Along the way, I authored peer-reviewed publications, served as principal investigator on federal research grants, and worked with partners like the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs and NVIDIA. That experience showed me both the potential for innovation in kidney care—and the structural barriers that hold it back.

What began as a simple newsletter quickly grew into a platform to amplify voices, surface insights, and connect people working across the “Kidneyverse.” Today, Signals advances kidney health through its media, research, and network—helping bring more visibility, collaboration, and innovation to the field.

We are joined by over 20,000 monthly readers in 55 countries, including many world class teams from industry, academia, government, and beyond.
